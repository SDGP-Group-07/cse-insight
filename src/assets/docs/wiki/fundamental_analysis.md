# How to Research a Company: The Fundamental Framework

Before investing your hard-earned money in a company on the **Colombo Stock Exchange (CSE)**, you need to look under the hood. While charts tell you *when* to buy, **Fundamental Analysis** tells you *what* to buy.

This guide follows the professional research framework developed by Brian Feroldi to help you separate high-quality businesses from risky gambles.

---

## Phase 1: The Business "Vibe Check"
If you don't understand how a company makes money, you shouldn't own it. Start with qualitative research.

* **What does the company do?** Can you explain their business model to a 10-year-old? For example: *"They bake bread and sell it to grocery stores across Sri Lanka"*.
* **Competitive Advantage (The "Moat"):** What makes them special? Is it a famous brand name, a lower price point, or a strategic government license?.
* **Industry Trend:** Is the sector growing (like Renewable Energy) or shrinking? It is much easier to grow in a rising industry.
* **Management:** Who is in charge? Look for leaders with a clean track record and a history of navigating economic shifts.

---

## Phase 2: The "Health Check" (Financial Statements)
Once you like the business model, you must check the company's "medical records"—their financial statements found in the **Annual Report**.

### 1. The Income Statement (The "Paystub")
This shows if the company is making a profit over a specific period of time.
* **Revenue Growth:** Are they selling more than they did last year?.
* **Profit Margins:** After paying for ingredients, labor, and taxes, how much "change" is left in the company's pocket?.

### 2. The Balance Sheet (The "Wallet")
A snapshot of what the company **owns** (Assets) vs. what it **owes** (Liabilities).
* **Cash:** How much "emergency money" do they have for a rainy day?.
* **Debt:** Do they owe more than they can realistically pay back?.
* **Equity:** This is the actual "Net Worth" of the company (Total Assets minus Total Debt).

### 3. Cashflow Statement (The "Bank Account")
This shows the actual physical movement of cash in and out of the business.
* **Free Cash Flow (FCF):** This is the most important number. It is the cold, hard cash left over after the company pays for its operations and maintains its buildings or machinery.



---

## Phase 3: The "Deep Dive" (Ratios & Risks)
Numbers mean more when you compare them to the company's size or past performance.

### Key Performance Ratios
* **Return on Equity (ROE):** This measures how much profit the company generates with the money shareholders have invested. **Target:** Look for **ROE > 15%**.
* **Net Margin:** This shows what percentage of every Rupee earned is actual profit. **Target:** Look for **Net Margin > 10%**.

### Identifying Key Risks
Every investment has "monsters" under the bed. You must identify them early:
* **Concentration Risk:** Does the company rely on just one big customer? If that customer leaves, the business might collapse.
* **Competition Risk:** Is a bigger, cheaper rival about to enter the market?.
* **Disruption Risk:** Is new technology (like AI or Automation) making their current product obsolete?.

---

## Phase 4: The Price Tag (Valuation)
Even a great company is a bad investment if you pay too much for it.

* **P/E Ratio (Price-to-Earnings):** This tells you how many "years" of profit you are paying upfront to own the stock today.
* **P/FCF Ratio:** This is similar to P/E, but it uses **Free Cash Flow** instead of accounting profit. It is often a more "honest" look at the company's value.

---

## Summary: The Decision
1.  **Understand** the business.
2.  **Verify** its health (Financial Statements).
3.  **Check** its efficiency (Ratios).
4.  **Confirm** the price (Valuation).

Once you have completed this workflow, you can make an informed decision to **BUY** or **SELL**.
