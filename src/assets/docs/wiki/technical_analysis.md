# Technical Analysis Gauge: A User Guide

In the **Colombo Stock Exchange (CSE)**, technical analysis can often feel overwhelming due to the sheer number of indicators available. The **Technical Analysis Gauge** is a powerful aggregation tool that simplifies this process. Instead of forcing you to study dozens of individual charts, it processes 26 popular technical indicators simultaneously to give you a "Consensus View" of a stock's current momentum.

This widget acts as a "Market Jury," where different technical "witnesses" (like Moving Averages and Oscillators) vote on whether a stock is currently a Buy, Sell, or Neutral.

---

## Understanding the "Voters"

The gauge is divided into three distinct categories, each looking at the market through a different lens.

### 1. The Summary (The Final Verdict)
This is the main "Speedometer" you see at the top. It combines the results of all 26 indicators into one final signal. 
* **Strong Buy/Sell:** Indicates a powerful, high-conviction trend.
* **Neutral:** Suggests a "sideways" market where neither bulls nor bears have control.

### 2. Oscillators (Momentum & Trend Strength)
These indicators (like **RSI**, **Stochastics**, and **MACD**) look for "Overbought" or "Oversold" conditions. They are best at telling you if a trend is getting "tired" and might reverse soon.

### 3. Moving Averages (The Trend Followers)
This section looks at the price relative to various timeframes (e.g., 10-day, 50-day, and 200-day averages). If the price is above most of its moving averages, it signals a healthy, long-term upward trend.

---

## Practical Comparison: Single Indicator vs. Gauge Consensus

Why is a "Consensus" better than looking at just one number? Here is how the Technical Analysis Gauge provides a more balanced view:

| Feature | Single Indicator (e.g., RSI Only) | Gauge Consensus (Aggregation) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Reliability** | Can give "False Signals" during news spikes. | More reliable; filters out the "noise" of one bad indicator. |
| **Complexity** | High; you must interpret the value yourself. | Low; gives a clear "Buy/Sell" summary. |
| **Context** | Only looks at one mathematical formula. | Looks at 26 different formulas across multiple timeframes. |
| **Market View** | A "Keyhole" view of the market. | A "Panoramic" view of the market. |
| **Summary** | Good for specialists. | Best for general traders and decision-making. |



---

## How to Use the Gauge Intervals

The gauge allows you to change the **Time Interval** (e.g., 1-minute, 1-hour, 1-day, or 1-week). This is crucial for matching your trading strategy:

* **Day Traders:** Focus on the **1-minute to 15-minute** intervals to catch quick "scalps."
* **Swing Traders:** Focus on the **1-hour to 4-hour** intervals for trends lasting a few days.
* **Position Investors:** Stick to the **1-day and 1-week** intervals to see the "Big Picture" for long-term holdings.

---

## Pro-Tips for Better Technical Accuracy
* **The "Alignment" Rule:** The strongest signals occur when the **Summary**, **Oscillators**, and **Moving Averages** all point in the same direction (e.g., all "Buy").
* **Beware the "Neutral" Zone:** When the needle is stuck in the middle, it often means the market is waiting for news. Avoid large entries during a Neutral phase in the CSE.
* **Check the Volume:** A "Strong Buy" signal is much more powerful if the trading volume for that stock is also increasing.

> **Note for CSE Investors:** In 2026, the CSE can experience sudden "Gaps" in price due to overnight economic announcements. While the Technical Analysis Gauge is excellent at showing *current* momentum, always combine it with the **AI Stock Engine's** "Confidence Score" to see if the engine believes the current trend is sustainable or likely to reverse tomorrow.
