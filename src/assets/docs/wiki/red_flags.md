# Red Flags to Avoid: A User Guide

In the **Colombo Stock Exchange (CSE)**, protecting your capital is just as important as growing it. While every investor looks for the next big winner, successful pros spend an equal amount of time looking for "Red Flags"—warning signs that a company or a stock's price movement is unstable, dishonest, or dangerous.

Learning to spot these signals early can save you from the "Value Traps" and "Pump and Dump" schemes that often catch retail investors off guard.

---

## 🚩 Major Warning Signs in Company Data

Before you buy, look for these three critical indicators that the "Engine" of the company might be failing.

### 1. The "Debt Trap" (Rising Finance Costs)
If a company’s debt is growing faster than its profit, it is in trouble.
* **The Red Flag:** A **Debt-to-Equity ratio** consistently above 2.0 or 3.0 in non-banking sectors.
* **Why it matters:** In Sri Lanka, high interest rates can quickly turn a profitable company into a loss-making one if they owe too much to the banks.

### 2. Sudden Auditor Resignations or Delayed Reports
Transparency is the foundation of trust.
* **The Red Flag:** If a company repeatedly delays its quarterly filings or if its independent auditor resigns suddenly.
* **Why it matters:** This often suggests "Accounting Irregularities" or that the company is trying to hide bad news from the public.

### 3. Excessive Related-Party Transactions
* **The Red Flag:** When a listed company constantly lends money or sells assets to other private companies owned by the same directors.
* **Why it matters:** This is often a way to move "Shareholder Wealth" into "Private Pockets."

---

## 📉 Spotting "Pump and Dump" Schemes

Not all price rises are healthy. Sometimes, a stock price is artificially pushed up so "Big Players" can sell their shares to unsuspecting "Small Players" at the top.



| Feature | Healthy Price Growth | "Pump and Dump" Red Flag |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Trigger** | Positive earnings or a new contract. | No news; only "rumors" on social media. |
| **Volume** | Steady increase in trading activity. | Massive, unexplained volume spike in 1-2 days. |
| **Price Action** | Consistent "Staircase" move up. | "Vertical" rocket-ship move with no pullbacks. |
| **Fundamentals** | Improving P/E and Profit margins. | Losing money, but the price is still doubling. |
| **Summary** | Built on **Business Success**. | Built on **Artificial Hype**. |

---

## Behavioral Red Flags
* **"FOMO" (Fear Of Missing Out):** If you feel an urgent need to buy a stock because "everyone else is making money," you are likely buying at the peak. 
* **The "Penny Stock" Trap:** Just because a stock is LKR 2.00 doesn't mean it's cheap. If the company is failing, it can still go to LKR 0.50—a 75% loss.
* **The "Guru" Tip:** If someone on a Telegram or WhatsApp group is telling you a stock is "guaranteed to double," they are likely looking for someone to buy the shares they are trying to sell.

---

> **Note for CSE Investors:** Be wary of "Speculative Runs" in small-cap companies with low liquidity. Because these companies have very few shares available, a small group of traders can easily manipulate the price. If a stock with zero profit is consistently on the "Top Gainers" list for a week, it is a major red flag. Always check the **AI Stock Engine** for the "Confidence Score" before joining a trend.
