# What Stockbrokers Do: More Than Just "Buying and Selling"

Think of a **Stockbroker** as your professional "Agent" for the world of finance. Since you cannot walk directly into the **Colombo Stock Exchange (CSE)** building and buy shares yourself, you need a licensed intermediary to do it for you. In 2026, a broker is much more than a person on a phone—they are your digital gateway, your research lab, and your safety net.

A broker’s job is to bridge the gap between your bank account and the stock market's high-speed trading engine.

---

## The Three Main Roles of a Broker

### 1. The Gatekeeper (Order Execution)
The most basic role is taking your "Order" (e.g., "Buy 100 shares of JKH") and sending it to the CSE's **Automated Trading System (ATS)**. They ensure the trade happens at the best possible price according to the rules.

### 2. The Librarian (Research & Analysis)
Modern brokers in Sri Lanka provide "Research Reports." They employ experts to analyze companies like **Hayleys** or **Commercial Bank** and tell you whether they think the stock will go up or down. This helps you make decisions based on facts rather than rumors.

### 3. The Custodian (Record Keeping)
Every time you buy a stock, your broker works with the **Central Depository System (CDS)** to make sure your name is legally recorded as the owner. They also help you manage your dividends and tax documents.

## Practical Comparison: Full-Service vs. Online Brokers

In 2026, most brokers in Sri Lanka offer a "Hybrid" model, but here is how the two styles of service differ:

| Feature | Full-Service Approach | Online/App-Only Approach |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Trading Method** | You call an investment advisor to place trades. | You use a mobile app or web portal. |
| **Personal Advice** | High. They suggest specific stocks for you. | Low. You make your own choices. |
| **Ideal For** | Beginners or busy professionals. | Active day traders and "Tech-savvy" users. |
| **Speed** | Slower (Wait for a phone call). | Instant (One-click execution). |
| **Summary** | Like having a **Private Chauffeur**. | Like driving your own **Sports Car**. |



---

## The "Fiducary" Responsibility
A stockbroker has a legal and ethical duty to act in your best interest. They are regulated by the **Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC)**. If a broker intentionally gives you bad advice just to make a commission, they can lose their license. This layer of protection is why you should *only* trade through licensed firms listed on the official CSE website.
