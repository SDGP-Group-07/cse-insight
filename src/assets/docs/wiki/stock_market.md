# What is a Stock Exchange?: A Beginner's Guide

To understand the **Colombo Stock Exchange (CSE)**, you first need to understand the concept of a "Stock Exchange" itself. At its simplest level, a stock exchange is a centralized marketplace where buyers and sellers meet to trade **Securities**, such as shares of companies or government bonds.

Think of it as a bridge: on one side are companies that need money to grow, and on the other side are investors who have money and want to grow it by owning a piece of those companies.

---

## The Two Main Functions

A stock exchange serves two vital purposes in a country's financial system:

### 1. Capital Raising (For Companies)
When a company wants to build a new factory or expand to a new country, they need a large amount of money. Instead of just taking a bank loan, they can "go public" and sell shares to thousands of investors. This is called **Primary Market** activity.

### 2. Liquidity & Trading (For Investors)
If you buy shares in a company, you don't want to be stuck with them forever. The stock exchange provides a **Secondary Market** where you can sell those shares to another investor in seconds. This "Liquidity" is what makes the stock market an attractive place to store wealth.

## Practical Comparison: Private vs. Public Companies

Understanding how a company changes once it joins an exchange like the CSE is key to evaluating its risk:

| Feature | Private Company | Publicly Listed Company (on CSE) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Ownership** | Owned by a few individuals or a family. | Owned by thousands of "Shareholders." |
| **Transparency** | Financial records are secret. | Must publish audited reports every 3 months. |
| **Buying/Selling** | Very difficult; requires legal contracts. | Very easy; can be done in one click on an app. |
| **Regulation** | Minimal government oversight. | Strictly regulated by the **SEC** and the **CSE**. |
| **Summary** | A "Closed Door" business. | An "Open Door" business accountable to the public. |

## Why Stock Exchanges Are Important
* **Economic Growth:** By helping companies get the money they need, stock exchanges create jobs and drive innovation.
* **Wealth Distribution:** Anyone, from a student to a retiree, can own a piece of a giant company like **John Keells** or **Commercial Bank** for a very small amount of money.
* **Safety & Trust:** Stock exchanges have strict rules. If a company lies about its profits, it faces heavy fines or "delisting," which protects your hard-earned money.

> **Note for CSE Investors:** The Colombo Stock Exchange is an **Order-Driven Market**. This means the price is determined entirely by the "Bids" (buyers) and "Asks" (sellers) in the system. There is no central person setting the price; it is a pure reflection of how much Sri Lankan and international investors believe a company is worth at any given second.
