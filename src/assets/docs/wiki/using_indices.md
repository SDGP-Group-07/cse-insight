# Using Indices: Understanding ASPI & S&P SL20

In the **Colombo Stock Exchange (CSE)**, you don't always have time to check every single stock. **Market Indices** act as the "Thermometer" of the economy. They tell you at a glance whether the overall market is "Healthy" (Green) or "Sick" (Red).

If you want to know how the "Market" is doing, you look at the **ASPI** and the **S&P SL20**. Think of them as the pulse of Sri Lanka’s financial heart.

---

## 1. ASPI (All Share Price Index)
* **ELI5:** The "Average Grade" of every student in the school.
* **What it is:** The ASPI measures the price movements of **all** companies listed on the CSE. If a small company in the plantation sector goes up and a giant bank goes down, they both affect this number.
* **Why it matters:** It is the broadest measure of the Sri Lankan equity market. When news says "The CSE rose by 50 points," they are usually talking about the ASPI.



---

## 2. S&P SL20 (The Elite 20)
* **ELI5:** The "Honors Class" or the "First XI Cricket Team."
* **What it is:** This index only tracks the **20 largest and most liquid** companies on the exchange. To be in this list, a company must be big, profitable, and have shares that are easy to buy and sell.
* **Why it matters:** This index represents the "Blue Chips." It is what foreign institutional investors and big pension funds (like the EPF) watch most closely. If the SL20 is down but the ASPI is up, it means small companies are doing well, but the giants are struggling.



---

## Practical Comparison: ASPI vs. S&P SL20

Understanding which index to follow depends on what kind of investor you are:

| Feature | ASPI (All Share) | S&P SL20 (The Elite) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Number of Stocks** | ~280+ (Every listed company). | Exactly 20. |
| **Weighting** | Market-Cap Weighted. | Market-Cap Weighted (Capped). |
| **Rebalancing** | Continuous. | Rebalanced twice a year (June/Dec). |
| **Main Focus** | Overall Market Sentiment. | Institutional & Blue-Chip Health. |
| **Volatility** | Moderate. | Can be higher (due to heavy bank weight). |
| **Summary** | The "Big Picture" of the CSE. | The "Power Players" of the CSE. |

---

## How to Use Indices in Your Analysis

1.  **Market Direction:** If both indices are moving up, the market is in a "Confirmed Bull Run." If they are moving in opposite directions, the market is "Confused" or "Divergent."
2.  **Benchmarking:** If your personal portfolio grew by 5% this month, but the ASPI grew by 10%, you have "Underperformed" the market. You need to adjust your strategy.
3.  **Sector Rotation:** Often, the SL20 (Banks/Conglomerates) will move first, followed by the smaller stocks in the ASPI. Use this delay to spot "catch-up" opportunities.



---

> **Note for CSE Investors:** In 2026, the S&P SL20 is heavily influenced by the **Banking and Finance** sector. Because these 20 companies are the "Systemically Important" ones, any change in Central Bank interest rates will usually hit the SL20 much faster than the rest of the ASPI. If you see the SL20 dropping while the ASPI stays flat, it's often a sign that big investors are "De-risking" before a major economic announcement.
