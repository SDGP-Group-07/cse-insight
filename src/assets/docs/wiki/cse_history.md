# CSE History and Structure: The Foundation of Sri Lankan Capital

The **Colombo Stock Exchange (CSE)** is the only licensed stock exchange in Sri Lanka. It serves as the primary gateway for companies to raise capital and for investors to participate in the country’s economic growth. Understanding its history and how it is organized helps you appreciate the high level of regulation and safety that protects your investments.

From a small room of brokers in the 1800s to a fully automated digital exchange in 2026, the CSE has evolved into one of the most technologically advanced markets in South Asia.

---

## A Timeline of Evolution

The CSE didn't start in a high-tech office. It began with the growing tea and rubber industries of the 19th century.

* **1896:** The **Share Brokers Association (SBA)** was formed to trade shares in plantation companies.
* **1985:** The SBA was officially incorporated as the **Colombo Securities Exchange**, later renamed the Colombo Stock Exchange in 1990.
* **1991:** The **Central Depository System (CDS)** was launched, ending the era of physical paper share certificates.
* **1995:** The CSE moved its headquarters to the **World Trade Center (WTC)** in Colombo, which remains its iconic home in 2026.
* **2020-2022:** The exchange underwent a total **Digital Transformation**, allowing for 100% paperless account opening and trading.

## The Regulatory Framework: Who is in Charge?

To ensure fairness, the CSE operates under a "Two-Tier" regulatory system. This structure ensures that no single person or company can "fix" the market.

| Entity | Role in the Market | Responsibilities |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **SEC (Securities & Exchange Commission)** | The **Regulator** (Government). | Grants licenses, protects investors, and punishes illegal activities like "Insider Trading." |
| **CSE (Colombo Stock Exchange)** | The **Operator** (Marketplace). | Manages the trading platform, lists new companies, and monitors daily price movements. |
| **CDS (Central Depository System)** | The **Safe/Vault** (Digital). | Keeps a digital record of who owns which shares. It is a subsidiary of the CSE. |
| **Stockbrokers** | The **Intermediaries**. | These are the licensed firms (like Enterprise Ceylon Capital) that execute trades for you. |



---

## How the CSE is Structured Internally
The CSE is a company "Limited by Guarantee," meaning it is owned by its **15 Member Firms**. It is governed by a Board of Directors consisting of five directors elected by the members and four directors appointed by the Government of Sri Lanka (via the Ministry of Finance). 

This balanced board ensures that the exchange serves both the interests of the brokers and the broader economic goals of the country.

---

> **Note for CSE Investors:** Because the CSE is a "Self-Regulatory Organization" (SRO), it has its own set of **Listing Rules**. If a company fails to disclose its profits or hides a major loss, the CSE can "Halt" or "Suspend" the stock to protect you from trading on unfair information. Always check the **"General Announcements"** section on the CSE website to see if your companies are in good standing.
