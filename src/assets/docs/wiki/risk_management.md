# Risk Management: A User Guide

In the **Colombo Stock Exchange (CSE)**, the difference between a successful veteran and a beginner who loses their savings is not how much they win, but how they manage their losses. **Risk Management** is the process of identifying, analyzing, and accepting or mitigating uncertainty in your investment decisions.

In trading, "Offense" is how you make money, but "Defense" (Risk Management) is how you keep it. Without a defensive plan, one bad trade can wipe out months of progress.

---

## The Core Tools of Risk Management

Professional traders never enter a trade without knowing exactly how much they are willing to lose.

### 1. The Stop-Loss Order
A stop-loss is a predetermined price at which you will automatically sell your stock to prevent further losses.
* **Why it matters:** It removes the emotion from the decision. If you buy at LKR 100 and set a stop-loss at LKR 90, you have capped your loss at 10%, no matter how far the market crashes.

### 2. Position Sizing (The 2% Rule)
This is the most powerful math in investing. It states that you should never risk more than **2% of your total account value** on any single trade.
* **Example:** If you have LKR 100,000, you should only risk LKR 2,000 per trade. This way, you would need to be wrong 50 times in a row to go broke.



### 3. Risk-to-Reward Ratio
Before buying, calculate if the potential gain is worth the risk. 
* **The Standard:** Aim for a **1:3 Ratio**. This means for every LKR 1 you risk, you aim to make LKR 3. 
* **The Math:** With a 1:3 ratio, you can be wrong 60% of the time and still make a profit!

## Practical Comparison: Gambling vs. Risk-Managed Trading

How you approach a trade determines whether you are an investor or a gambler.

| Feature | Gambling (No Risk Plan) | Risk-Managed Trading |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Trade Size** | Puts all money into one "hot" stock. | Splits money into 5–10 calculated positions. |
| **Exit Strategy** | "Hopes" the price goes up. | Has a clear Stop-Loss and Take-Profit target. |
| **Emotional State** | Panics when the price drops 5%. | Remains calm; the loss was already planned. |
| **Account Growth** | Huge spikes followed by "Blowing the account." | Slow, steady, and sustainable growth. |
| **Summary** | Relies on **Luck**. | Relies on **Mathematics and Probability**. |

## The Three Types of Risk in the CSE
* **Market Risk:** The risk that the entire ASPI index drops due to national news.
* **Sector Risk:** The risk that a specific industry (like Tourism) faces a setback while others are fine.
* **Liquidity Risk:** The risk that a stock has so few buyers that you cannot sell your shares when you want to without dropping the price.



---

> **Note for CSE Investors:** In the Sri Lankan market, **Liquidity Risk** is a major factor. Many "Penny Stocks" (stocks trading under LKR 5) can jump 20% in a day, but when you try to sell your 50,000 shares, there may not be enough buyers. **Risk Management Tip:** Avoid putting more than 5% of your portfolio into "Illiquid" stocks, even if the Market Intelligence Terminal shows a Bullish bias. Stick to "Liquid" stocks for your core holdings so you can exit quickly if the trend changes.
