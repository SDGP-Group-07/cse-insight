# Brokerage Fees Structure: Understanding Transaction Costs

In the **Colombo Stock Exchange (CSE)**, transaction costs are transparent and strictly regulated. As of the latest update (effective **July 28, 2025**), the fee structure is designed to be fair for small retail investors while offering a "Step-up" benefit for those moving larger volumes of capital.

Think of these fees as the "Fuel" that keeps the market’s digital engine running—paying for the brokers, the regulators, and the secure vaults where your shares are kept.

---

## 1. Equity Transactions: The 1.12% Rule

For most individual investors making trades up to **LKR 100 Million**, the total cost is fixed at **1.12%** of the transaction value. This applies to both buying and selling.

### The Breakdown of the 1.12%
When you pay LKR 1.12 for every LKR 100 you trade, that money is split among several institutions to ensure a safe and efficient market:

| Fee Component | Rate (%) | Who Receives It? |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Brokerage Fees** | 0.640% | Your Stockbroker (for app, research, and service). |
| **CSE Fees** | 0.084% | The Exchange (for the electronic trading system). |
| **CDS Fees** | 0.012% | The Central Depository (the digital share vault). |
| **Clearing Fees** | 0.012% | CSE Clear (for finalizing the trade). |
| **SEC Cess** | 0.072% | The Regulator (to ensure market fairness). |
| **Share Transaction Levy** | 0.300% | The Government (Direct Tax). |



---

## 2. Large Trades: The "Step-up" Basis

For massive transactions over **LKR 100 Million**, the CSE uses a "Step-up" calculation. You don't pay the lower rate on the whole amount; you only pay it on the portion that *exceeds* the 100M threshold.

* **First LKR 100 Million:** Always charged at **1.12%**.
* **Balance over 100 Million:** Charged at approximately **0.6125%** (assuming a negotiated brokerage of 0.2%).

**Example: A LKR 102 Million Trade**
1. First 100 Million @ 1.12% = LKR 1,120,000
2. Remaining 2 Million @ 0.6125% = LKR 12,250
3. **Total Cost:** **LKR 1,132,250**

> **Note:** For trades of **LKR 1 Billion and above**, the brokerage fee is fully negotiable as per the SEC directive of March 2024.

---

## 3. The "Intraday" Advantage: Trading for Half-Price

If you are a high-speed trader who buys and sells the same stock on the same day through the same broker, the CSE offers a significant discount. 

**The Rule:** You are only charged the full transaction fee on **one side** of the trade (the larger quantity). 
* **The Exemption:** Fees (Brokerage, CSE, CDS, Clearing, and SEC) are waived on the lower quantity of shares traded.
* **The Exception:** The **Share Transaction Levy (0.300%)** must always be paid on *both* sides (Buy and Sell).

**Example:**
* You buy 5,000 shares of ABC.
* You sell 4,000 shares of ABC later that same day.
* **Result:** You pay full fees on the 5,000 bought, but you **only pay the Levy** on the 4,000 sold. The other fees are waived!



---

## 4. Other Securities: Corporate Debt & Funds

Not everything on the CSE is a common stock. Different rules apply to Debt and Funds:

### Corporate Debt (Bonds)
* **Brokerage:** Negotiable.
* **Institutions (SEC, CSE, CDS):** 1 Basis Point (divided equally).
* **REPO Transactions:** A fixed 0.02% per annum split between the broker and institutions.

### Closed-End Funds
* **Share Transaction Levy:** Not Applicable (0%).
* **Brokerage:** Negotiable (capped at 1%).
* **Institutional Fees:** Approximately 0.05% total.

---

> **Note for CSE Investors:** Understanding these costs is vital for calculating your "Break-even" point. Because you pay 1.12% to buy and 1.12% to sell, your stock usually needs to rise by at least **2.24%** before you are in the green. Always check the **Transaction Cost** page on `cse.lk` for the most recent updates to these statutory rates.
