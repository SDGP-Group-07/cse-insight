# Portfolio Diversification: A User Guide

In the **Colombo Stock Exchange (CSE)**, the most common mistake retail investors make is "falling in love" with a single stock and putting all their money into it. **Portfolio Diversification** is the scientific way to manage risk. It is the practice of spreading your investments across different companies, industries, and asset classes so that one bad event doesn't wipe out your entire savings.

As the old saying goes: "Don't put all your eggs in one basket." If that basket drops, you lose everything. Diversification gives you multiple baskets.

---

## The Three Layers of a Protected Portfolio

To build a "Bulletproof" portfolio, you should diversify at three different levels.



### 1. Asset Allocation (The Big Picture)
Don't just own stocks. A balanced investor in Sri Lanka typically holds a mix of:
* **Equities (Stocks):** For long-term growth.
* **Fixed Income:** Treasury Bills or Fixed Deposits for guaranteed safety.
* **Gold/Real Estate:** To protect against LKR inflation.

### 2. Sector Diversification (The Industry Split)
Different industries react differently to economic news. 
* **Example:** If global oil prices rise, **Manufacturing** companies might see higher costs (bad), but **Renewable Energy** companies might see higher demand (good). By owning both, your portfolio stays stable.

### 3. Individual Stock Weighting (The 10% Rule)
Even if you love a company like JKHL or COMB, never let a single stock represent more than **10% to 15%** of your total portfolio. 
* **Why?** Even the best-managed companies can face unexpected "Black Swan" events like a factory fire or a sudden change in management.

---

## Practical Comparison: Concentrated vs. Diversified Portfolio

Here is how diversification protects your wealth during a typical market "correction" in the CSE:

| Feature | Concentrated Portfolio (1-2 Stocks) | Diversified Portfolio (8-12 Stocks) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Risk Level** | Extremely High (All-or-Nothing). | Moderate (Protected by variety). |
| **Impact of One Bad News** | A 20% drop in one stock ruins your year. | A 20% drop in one stock is only a 2% total loss. |
| **Emotional Stress** | High. You panic at every price tick. | Low. You trust the overall "Team" effort. |
| **Growth Potential** | Can be very high if you are lucky. | Consistent and steady over time. |
| **Summary** | Like "Gambling" on a single horse. | Like "Owning" the entire race track. |



---

## The Danger of "Over-Diversification"
While spreading risk is good, owning **too many** stocks (e.g., 50 different small companies) is also a mistake. 
* **The "Diworsification" Trap:** If you own too many stocks, you won't have time to research them all, and your "winners" won't have enough weight to actually make you rich. 
* **The Sweet Spot:** For most CSE investors, owning **8 to 12 strong companies** across 4 different sectors is the perfect balance.

---

> **Note for CSE Investors:** The Sri Lankan market is heavily weighted toward **Banks, Finance, and Insurance**. If you only own banking stocks, you are NOT diversified, even if you own 5 different banks! To be truly protected, ensure your portfolio also includes "Defensive" sectors like **Utilities, Consumer Staples (Food/Healthcare), or Export-oriented** companies that earn in USD.
