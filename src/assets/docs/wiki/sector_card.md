# Decoding the Sector Card: CSE Insight Guide

This guide explains the institutional metrics used on the sector dashboard. These values help you decide whether an industry looks like a growth opportunity, a value bargain, or a safe haven.

---

## 1. The Pulse (Header)

The top section shows the **Index Value**. Think of this as the "temperature" of the industry.

* **Index Value:** The collective price of all stocks in the sector.
* **Percentage Change:** If it is green (`▲`), the sector gained value today. If it is red (`▼`), it lost value.
* **SPCSEAMP:** The S&P industry group code. This is the official naming format used by professional traders.

---

## 2. Market Breadth (Sentiment Bar)

This tells you whether the move is a team effort or just one company moving the needle.

* **ADV (Advancers):** Companies that finished higher than yesterday.
* **DEC (Decliners):** Companies that finished lower.
* **UNC (Unchanged):** Companies that did not move.
* **ELI5 insight:** If the bar is mostly red but the Index is green, one large company may be hiding weakness in most of the sector.

---

## 3. Valuation (Price Tag Logic)

This helps you spot whether an industry is cheap or expensive relative to what it earns.

* **P/E Ratio (Price-to-Earnings):** How many years of profit you are paying for upfront.
* **Low (`< 8x`):** Potentially a bargain (or a struggling industry).
* **High (`> 20x`):** Investors are pricing in strong future growth.
* **P/B Ratio (Price-to-Book):** Compares stock price to a company's net assets (buildings, cash, and other balance-sheet value).
* **`1x` example:** You are buying the company for roughly what its assets are worth.
* **Dividend Yield:** Your annual cash return.
* **`7.5%` example:** For every LKR 100 invested, you receive about LKR 7.50 per year in dividends.

---

## 4. Insights (Deep Data)

* **Market Cap:** The total weight of the sector. `6.99B` means the sector is worth 6.99 billion rupees.
* **Wtd. Beta (Risk Meter):** Measures how much the sector moves compared to the broader market.
* **Beta `< 1.0`:** Defensive (typically less volatile).
* **Beta `> 1.0`:** Aggressive (typically more volatile).
* **Companies (`1/1`):** Indicates how diversified the sector is. In this example, `TYRE.N0000` represents the sector.

---

## 5. Top Contributor (MVP)

This shows which company had the highest trading action today.

* **VOL (Volume):** Total number of shares traded. `1.3M` signals strong buyer-seller activity in that stock.
