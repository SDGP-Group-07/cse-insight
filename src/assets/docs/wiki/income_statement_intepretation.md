# Income Statement Interpretation: A User Guide

To succeed on the **Colombo Stock Exchange (CSE)**, you must look past the daily stock price and focus on the "Engine" of the business. The **Income Statement** (also known as the Profit & Loss Statement) shows how much money a company made and how much it spent over a specific period—usually a quarter or a year.

While the Balance Sheet shows what a company *has*, the Income Statement shows what a company *does*. It tells the story of whether a business is growing, shrinking, or just barely breaking even.

---

## The Path to Profitability

The Income Statement is a "Waterfall." It starts with the total money coming in and subtracts costs until you reach the final profit.



### 1. Revenue (The Top Line)
This is the total amount of money a company received from selling its products or services.
* **ELI5:** If a shop sells 100 shirts for LKR 1,000 each, the Revenue is LKR 100,000.

### 2. Gross Profit & Operating Expenses
* **Gross Profit:** What’s left after paying for the direct cost of the goods sold (the fabric for the shirts).
* **Operating Expenses (OPEX):** The "overhead" costs like staff salaries, electricity, and marketing.

### 3. Net Profit (The Bottom Line)
This is the "Real" profit. It is what remains after every single expense, including taxes and interest on bank loans, has been paid. This is the money that can be used to pay you **Dividends**.

---

## Practical Comparison: High-Quality vs. Low-Quality Earnings

Not all profits are created equal. Here is how to distinguish between a company with "Sustainable" growth and one with "Artificial" gains:

| Feature | High-Quality Earnings | Low-Quality Earnings |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Source of Profit** | Selling more products/services (Core Business). | Selling a building or a one-time "Exchange Gain." |
| **Operating Margins** | Consistent or improving over time. | Fluctuating wildly or dropping every year. |
| **Expense Control** | Costs are growing slower than Revenue. | Costs are spiraling out of control. |
| **Growth Driver** | "Organic" (Capturing more market share). | Driven mostly by raising prices due to inflation. |
| **Summary** | The business is fundamentally healthy and scalable. | The profit looks good on paper, but it might disappear next year. |

---

## Why Traders Analyze the Income Statement
* **EPS (Earnings Per Share):** Traders divide the Net Profit by the number of shares to see how much "Value" each share is generating.
* **Margin Analysis:** By looking at the "Net Margin," you can see if the company is efficient. A company with a 20% margin is much stronger than one with a 2% margin.
* **Growth Trends:** Professional investors look at the "Year-over-Year" (YoY) growth to see if the company is expanding its footprint in the Sri Lankan market.

> **Note for CSE Investors:** In the current economic climate in Sri Lanka, **Finance Costs** (interest on loans) are a major factor. Always check the "Finance Cost" line on a CSE filing; a company might have great sales, but if their bank interest is too high, it will "eat" all the profit before it reaches the shareholders.
