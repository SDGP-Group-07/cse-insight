# Understanding Stock Charts: A Beginner's Guide

To navigate the **Colombo Stock Exchange (CSE)** effectively, you need to move beyond simple line graphs. The most powerful tool used by traders is the **Candlestick Chart**. 

While a line graph only shows the closing price, a candlestick tells the story of the "battle" between buyers and sellers throughout the trading session.

---

## Anatomy of a Candlestick

Each vertical bar on a chart is a **Candlestick**. It represents a specific time frame—usually one trading day.



### 1. The Candle Body (The Rectangular Part)
The "thick" part of the candle represents the price range between the **Open** (the price when the market started) and the **Close** (the price when the market ended).

* **Green Candle (Bullish):** Indicates the price went **UP**. The stock closed at a higher price than it opened.
* **Red Candle (Bearish):** Indicates the price went **DOWN**. The stock closed at a lower price than it opened.

### 2. The Wicks (The Thin Lines)
The thin lines poking out of the top and bottom are called **Wicks** or **Shadows**. They show the price "extremes" reached during the day.

* **Upper Wick:** The highest price the stock reached before falling back down.
* **Lower Wick:** The lowest price the stock touched before bouncing back up.

---

## Why Traders Use Candlesticks
* **Market Sentiment:** A candle with a tiny body and very long wicks suggests indecision in the market. 
* **Strength:** A large green body with almost no wicks suggests "Aggressive Buying"—the bulls were in total control from start to finish.
* **Reversal Signals:** Certain shapes (like a "Hammer") can alert investors that a falling stock might be about to start rising again.

> **Note for CSE Investors:** On the Colombo Stock Exchange, price movements are often driven by local news and dividend announcements. Watching these candles can help you see if the "big players" are buying or selling in response to that news.
