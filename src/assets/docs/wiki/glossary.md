# The Master A–Z Glossary: Colombo Stock Exchange (CSE)

This is your comprehensive "Cheat Sheet" for the Sri Lankan stock market. Whether you are reading an annual report or using the **Market Intelligence Terminal**, use this alphabetical guide to decode the jargon instantly.

---

## A
* **Alpha (α):** Your "Skill Score." It shows how much your portfolio beat the market average. 
* **ASPI (All Share Price Index):** The "Average Grade" of every company on the CSE.
* **Assets:** Everything a company owns (cash, buildings, inventory).

## B
* **Balance Sheet:** A "Snapshot" of what a company owns (Assets) vs. what it owes (Liabilities).
* **Bear Market:** A "Grumpy" market where prices are falling (like a bear swiping down).
* **Beta (β):** The "Mood Swing" score. High Beta means the stock jumps more than the market.
* **Bid-Ask Spread:** The "Haggling Gap" between the highest buyer price and lowest seller price.
* **Blue Chip:** The "Royalty" of the CSE. Large, safe, and famous companies like JKH or COMB.
* **Board Lot:** The "Minimum Packet." On the CSE, you can usually buy as little as 1 share.
* **Bonus Issue:** A "Loyalty Gift" where the company gives you extra shares for free.
* **Bull Market:** A "Happy" market where prices are charging up (like a bull thrusting up).


## C
* **Capital Gains:** The profit you make by selling a stock for more than you paid.
* **Capital Gains Tax (CGT):** The "Profit Tax" paid to the government on your gains.
* **Cash Flow:** The actual "Physical Cash" moving in and out of a business.
* **Compound Interest:** The "Snowball Effect" where your earnings earn their own earnings.
* **Corporate Disclosures:** Official "News Alerts" that companies must tell the public.
* **Current Ratio:** A safety check. Does the company have enough cash to pay its bills this year?

## D
* **Day Trading:** A "Sprint." Buying and selling the same stock within a single day.
* **Debt-to-Equity:** A "Stress Test." How much the company owes banks vs. how much it actually owns.
* **Diversification:** "Not putting all your eggs in one basket." Spreading money across different sectors.
* **Dividend:** The "Cashback" a company pays you from its profits.
* **Dividend Yield:** The "Interest Rate" of your stock. (Dividend ÷ Price).
* **DPS (Dividend Per Share):** The exact amount of rupees you get for each share you own.

## E
* **EPS (Earnings Per Share):** The "Pizza Slice." How much profit belongs to each single share.
* **EV/EBITDA:** The "Real Price" of a business including its debt.
* **Ex-Dividend (XD):** The "Deadline." You must own the stock *before* this date to get the dividend.

## F
* **Financial Quarter:** A 3-month "Report Card" for the company (Q1, Q2, Q3, Q4).
* **FOMO:** "Fear Of Missing Out." Buying a stock just because everyone else is talking about it.
* **Free Cash Flow (FCF):** The "Leftover Cash" a company has after paying all its bills.

## G
* **Gross Profit:** Money left after paying for the *direct* cost of making a product.
* **Growth Stocks:** "Race Cars." Companies that grow fast but usually don't pay dividends yet.

## I
* **IPO (Initial Public Offering):** The "Grand Opening." When a private company joins the CSE.
* **Intrinsic Value:** The "True Worth" of a company, regardless of its current market price.



## L
* **Liabilities:** Everything a company owes to others (loans, unpaid bills).
* **Liquidity:** How easy it is to "Cash Out" and turn your shares back into bank balance.

## M
* **MACD:** A tool that tracks "Momentum" to see if a trend is getting stronger or weaker.
* **Market Capitalization:** The total "Price Tag" of a company (Price × Total Shares).
* **Moving Average (MA):** A line that smooths out daily price wiggles to show the clear trend.

## O
* **Odd Lot:** Any trade that is a weird number (not a standard multiple).
* **Operating Profit:** The "Core Profit" from the company's actual daily business.

## P
* **P/B Ratio:** Comparing the stock price to the value of the company's physical "Stuff."
* **P/E Ratio:** The most common "Value Tool." How many years of profit you are paying for today.
* **Position Trading:** A "Marathon." Holding a stock for months or years.

## R
* **Relative Strength Index (RSI):** A 0-100 scale showing if a stock is "Overbought" or "Oversold."
* **Return on Equity (ROE):** How much profit the company makes using the shareholders' money.
* **Rights Issue:** When a company asks current owners for more money in exchange for discounted shares.

## S
* **S&P SL20:** The "Top 20" largest and most active companies on the CSE.
* **Share Split:** Cutting the "Pizza" into more slices to make the price look cheaper.
* **Short Selling:** Betting that a stock price will go *down*.
* **Stop-Loss:** An "Emergency Exit" order to sell automatically if the price drops too far.
* **Swing Trading:** Capturing price "Waves" that last 3 to 15 days.

## T
* **T+2 Settlement:** The "Waiting Period." Cash from a sale takes 2 business days to settle.
* **Technical Analysis:** Using charts and math to predict future price moves.

## V
* **Volatility:** How much a stock "Jumps" around. High volatility = High risk.
* **Volume:** The "Truth Meter." How many shares were actually traded today.

## W
* **Withholding Tax (WHT):** The tax the company takes from your dividend before paying you.
* **Working Capital:** The "Fuel." Money available for the company's daily operations.
