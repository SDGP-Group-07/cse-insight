# The Settlement Process (T+2): How Trades Are Finalized

In the **Colombo Stock Exchange (CSE)**, clicking "Buy" or "Sell" on your app is just the beginning. The actual exchange of money for shares doesn't happen instantly; it follows a regulated cycle known as **T+2 Settlement**. 

"T" stands for the **Trade Date** (the day you made the transaction), and "2" represents the **two business days** it takes for the ownership and the cash to officially change hands. 

---

## The T+2 Timeline: A Step-by-Step Breakdown

In 2026, the CSE operates on a strict T+2 schedule. Here is what happens behind the scenes during those 48 hours:

### Day T: The Trade Date
* **Action:** You place an order, and the **Automated Trading System (ATS)** matches it with a seller.
* **Result:** You receive a digital trade confirmation from your broker. The shares are "Locked" in the seller's account, and your cash is earmarked for the purchase.

### Day T+1: The Verification Day
* **Action:** The **Central Depository System (CDS)** and your broker firm review the transaction to ensure all details (price, quantity, and account numbers) are correct.
* **Result:** Final settlement instructions are sent to the **Settlement Banks**.

### Day T+2: The Settlement Date (The Exchange)
* **Action:** This is the day the "Real Swap" happens. 
    * **By 9:30 AM:** Buying brokers must ensure cleared funds are in the settlement bank.
    * **By 10:30 AM:** The CDS moves the shares from the Seller’s account to the Buyer’s account.
* **Result:** The seller receives their cash (minus commissions), and the buyer officially sees the new shares in their **CDS Cleared Balance**.



---

## Practical Comparison: Buyer vs. Seller Perspective

How the T+2 cycle affects your bank account and your portfolio:

| Feature | For the Buyer | For the Seller |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Share Ownership** | You can see the shares immediately, but they are "Unsettled" until T+2. | Shares are "Locked" on Day T; you can no longer sell them. |
| **Cash Movement** | Cash is usually deducted from your broker ledger on Day T or T+1. | Cash arrives in your account/ledger on the morning of **T+2**. |
| **Selling Again** | You can "Day Trade" or sell on T+1, even before the first trade is settled! | You must wait until T+2 to withdraw the cash to your bank. |
| **Dividends** | You must own the stock by the **XD Date** to be the "Owner of Record" on T+2. | You lose the right to future dividends as of the Trade Date (T). |

---

## Why Do We Wait Two Days?

You might wonder why it isn't instant in a digital world. The T+2 period serves as a **Safety Buffer**:
1.  **Risk Reduction:** It gives the system time to handle "Defaults" (e.g., if a buyer doesn't have enough money).
2.  **Accuracy:** It allows brokers to correct any manual entry errors before the money leaves the bank.
3.  **Liquidity:** It gives institutional investors (who move billions of Rupees) time to move funds between international accounts.



---

> **Note for CSE Investors:** The T+2 cycle counts **Business Days only**. If you sell a stock on a Friday, the "T+1" is Monday, and the "T+2" (when you get your money) is **Tuesday**. If there is a Public Holiday (like Poya Day) on Monday, the settlement is pushed to Wednesday. Always check the **CSE Holiday Calendar** to plan your cash withdrawals!
